What does my BMI say about my health?

As your body mass index (BMI) increases above 25, the more at risk you are for developing chronic diseases such as type 2 diabetes, heart disease, high blood pressure, stroke, certain types of cancers, high scholesterol, sleep apnea, osteoarthritis, gallbladder disease and more. BMI can produce altered results if an individual has a lot of muscle mass such as a body builder, as their BMI would suggest they are overweight. BMI can also be an inaccurate measurement for older adults who have week and brittle bones providing them with a BMI that actually doesn't represent all of the fat mass they have because their bones are not as heavy as a normal adults.
